THE WAY
I COPE

The Way I Cope is a visual identity and storytelling project exploring the influences that shaped me: family, nature, music, and design. The project uses typography, image-making, abstraction, and poster design to turn personal reflection into a visual system.

PROJECT IDEA

This project began with five personal motifs: brace, comedy, untamed, self-determination, and stunning. These ideas became the foundation for a series of posters and visual experiments.

PROCESS

I worked with expressive typography, scanned textures, photography, contrast, and layered compositions to create a rough, emotional, and personal visual style.
